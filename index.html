About

I am a PhD student in physics at MIT. My research focuses on theoretical and computational photonics, especially problems involving wave propagation, photonic systems, and physically implemented computation.

Previously, I studied physics at NTU and worked on research in photonics and related computational methods.

Research Interests

Integrated Photonics
On-chip optical systems, waveguide devices, and photonic circuits.
Optical Neural Networks
Physical implementations of computation and matrix operations using light.
Inverse Design
Optimization-based design of photonic structures and devices.
Computational Electromagnetics
Numerical methods for wave physics and electromagnetic modeling.

Selected Projects

Optical Neural Network Architectures
Exploring photonic implementations of structured matrix operations and physically realizable neural-network layers.
Adjoint-Based Inverse Design
Using gradient-based optimization methods to design and improve photonic devices.
